Date: 12/10/2025

Call to Order

Meeting called to order by Robert Keister (WZ5V). Welcome to all members and guests.

Pledge of Allegiance to the American Flag.

An opening prayer was offered by John McDaniel (KI5VXP) ("Papa John"), who gave thanks for the season and remembered recently lost club members and their families.

Remembrance

The club acknowledged the passing of Brian Belcher (WA5M) (November 14th) and his wife, who passed away seven days after him. Both were remembered for their dedication and contributions to the club. Additionally, the club mourned the loss of Randy Davis (KF5ZKU) (December 8, 2025), a valued member known for his participation and warm presence. Their lives and service were honored, and condolences were extended to their families.

Welcome and Member Updates

New and returning guests were welcomed, including Joe and his wife Brenda. Jeff was introduced as a new member candidate, and his wife was also present. The club inquired about new licensees and call signs; Jeff was noted as working on an upgrade.

Approval of Previous Meeting Minutes

The minutes from the previous meeting, as published in the Mark newsletter, were motioned for approval by Kyri Calder (KG5WXZ) and seconded by Tim Valis (KK5TIM). The motion carried without opposition.

David Hampton (KI5LPO), the VE coordinator, reported one person (Ellis Thompson) tested at the session. David's efforts and the support of the volunteer examiners were acknowledged.

Applications from Nicholas Haban (AF6CF) (Yorba Linda, California) and Jeff Rupp (KJ5NGO) (McKinney) were considered. The motion to approve their membership was made by Roy DeLeon (KB4ROY) and seconded by Bob DeVance (K5CRX). Both were welcomed as new members, and Jeff was invited to collect his name badge.

The treasurer's report indicated a starting balance of $23,367.54, contributions totaling $288.00, and expenses just over $3.00, resulting in an ending balance of $23,652.18. Members were invited to ask questions; the club maintains transparency in financial matters.

Repeater Update

Ron provided updates on repeater projects, highlighting progress at Fairview and ongoing coordination with town officials. He noted the recent loss of Brian Belcher, who was instrumental in establishing the club's repeater infrastructure. Developments in Melissa include a city-mandated maintenance project requiring temporary relocation of antennas, with efforts underway to negotiate logistics and costs. Other repeater sites, including 75/380 and Custer/Virginia, were briefly discussed.

Robert expressed gratitude to Ron and all volunteers supporting technical operations, including Dick Price, Dave Pekarsky, Sid, and Luis. Their expertise and dedication were deeply appreciated.

Fox Hunting Activities

Despite Felicia being under the weather, Steve and Marco were active in fox hunting. Members were encouraged to participate in this fun and challenging activity.

A soldering class will be held at Fire Station #5 (6600 Virginia Parkway) on Saturday at 9 AM, led by Cliff (KE5VXZ) and supported by Bob DeVance (K5CRX). Members were encouraged to bring soldering tools, wire cutters, solder, and flux if available, as well as helping hands (circuit board stands). Information is also available on Discord.

Mike announced the "Elves on the Air" event starting tomorrow night, inviting members and their families to participate in on-air holiday activities using the special call sign November 5 Papa (North 5 Pole). Volunteers are needed for the "Holidays at the Herd" event (Friday‚ÄìSunday), assisting with shuttle bus monitoring and facilitating radio communication for children with "elves." Members were urged to sign up via Discord or provided links, wear club apparel and high-visibility vests, and be prepared for outdoor conditions. The club values this opportunity to give back, as the Herd Museum has supported club VE sessions for years.

The meeting continued with further announcements and concluded with gratitude to all attendees and volunteers. Motion made to adjourn by Mike Flusche (W5MEF), second by Larry Land (KI5OEX). Motion carried.

üìö MARC General License Class

A General License prep class will be offered on Thursday evenings from January 8 to February 12 to assist in preparation for MARC's testing session on Heard Museum in February 15. The one-hour classes will be presented on Zoom from 7:00 PM to 8:00 PM each Thursday.

The class will cover the technical portions of the exam. The sections on rules, regulations, and operation will not be covered in this class. There are several on-line exam prep and review options available ‚Äì many for free. These on-line programs are very useful for reviewing the rules portions of the Exam as well as all the other questions in the question pool.

The purpose of this class is to help hams get a better grasp of the technical sections of the Exam. There will be time for discussion of technical details related to the Exam questions. The General Exam requires 26 of 35 questions from a pool of 450 questions. 26 correct answers is the passing score.

Some of the topics to be covered are antennas & feed lines, digital electronics, Ohm's Law, electronic components, and question-based circuit analysis. We will be using the PowerPoint slide deck from Gordon West, W6BNO and the W5YI group. This deck follows West's General Class book. We will also reference the ARRL General Class book as well. I recommend either book as a great study reference.

The course is free.

The Zoom link is: https://us06web.zoom.us/j/88593241933
The passcode is: 517140

Ready to upgrade? Join our class to ramp up your readiness for the General Exam.

Bob, K5CRX, Instructor

üîß Building Skills: Soldering Class Highlights

MARC's December 13th soldering class at Fire Station #5 was a tremendous success! Led by Cliff Harden - KE5VXZ, with expert assistance from Bob DeVance - K5CRX, members gained hands-on experience building functional 12V battery monitor devices from scratch.

Participants learned the basics of proper soldering technique, including the differences between solder types, proper temperature control, and professional methods for creating reliable connections. Members brought their own soldering irons, wire cutters, solder, and flux, while the club provided helping hands (circuit board stands) for those who needed them. Everyone walked away with a completed battery monitor project and newfound confidence in their soldering abilities.

A heartfelt thank you to all the Elmers who make these educational sessions possible. Your patience, knowledge, and dedication to mentoring fellow members are invaluable to our club's mission of continuous learning and skill development!

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR ‚Äî GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services. Club members should utilize good judgment with regard to topics or content that are posted on the MARC reflector. To that end it is recommended that political and religious topics or content be avoided unless directly related to amateur radio. In addition, personal attacks in any form will not be tolerated.
