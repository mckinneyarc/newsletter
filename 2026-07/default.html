📢 President's Message

Welcome to the hot summer days of July!

Before we dive into the Texas heat, let's take a moment to look back at an incredible month for MARC.

Field Day was an outstanding success! We far exceeded last year's QSO total, and our dedicated CW operators were absolutely pounding the brass, making more than 500 contacts on their own. It certainly didn't hurt that we were treated to fantastic facilities this year, including the luxury of air conditioning! A heartfelt thank you goes to our Field Day Director, Mike Flusche (W5MEF), whose countless hours of planning and hard work made the event a tremendous success as well as all those who pitched in to make it all happen from the antenna setup to the tear-down and clean up. I'd also like to thank Lance Chapman (KM6LMP) for securing such an outstanding location for this year's event.

Field Day wasn't just about making contacts, it was also about growing our hobby. A huge thank you to David Hampton (KI5LPO) and our Volunteer Examiner team for conducting license exams during the event. Congratulations to the operators who earned their Amateur Extra licenses and to our newest Technician, who will officially be joining the club at our July meeting! Special recognition also goes to our own Bob DeVance, whose weekly Extra Class study sessions helped make many of those license upgrades possible.

Of course, no Field Day would be complete without a little friendly competition. Our Chili Cook-Off was another huge hit! Before the judging even began, a playful and perhaps slightly divisive debate broke out over one timeless Texas question: beans or no beans? Did we finally settle the matter once and for all? Not quite! We'll keep that discussion simmering and stir the pot again at our next meeting... all in good fun, of course!

Our MARC Equipment Auction was another tremendous success. Thanks to the generosity of our members, we raised more than $7,000 to support current and future club projects. Thank you to everyone who donated equipment, volunteered their time, and placed bids. We still have several items remaining, and those will be offered for public bidding in the near future. Watch for more details at this month's general meeting.

This year also marks a truly historic Independence Day as our nation celebrates the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence. It's a wonderful opportunity to reflect on what freedom means to each of us, our families, and our country. Independence Day reminds us of the courage and sacrifices of those who secured our liberty and of the enduring ideals of freedom, unity, hope, and opportunity that continue to shape our nation. As you celebrate with family and friends, I hope you'll take a moment to appreciate the many blessings we enjoy and the responsibility we all share in preserving them for future generations.

As we move deeper into the Texas summer, please remember to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and take precautions against the heat. We want every member and your family to enjoy a fun, safe, and healthy summer season.

Thank you, as always, for being a valued member of MARC. Your enthusiasm, volunteer spirit, and dedication are what make our club such a welcoming, active, and enjoyable community. I look forward to seeing everyone at our July meeting. Until then, stay cool, stay safe, and keep those radios on the air!

73,
— Robert Keister, WZ5V
2026 MARC President

🌍 Join Us!

The McKinney Amateur Radio Club (MARC) welcomes everyone, whether you're just discovering the world of Amateur Radio or you're an experienced operator looking to connect with fellow enthusiasts. Our club is open to anyone with a passion for radio communication, a curiosity for learning, or a desire to expand their skills. Amateur Radio is a fascinating, educational, and rewarding hobby, and we invite you to explore everything MARC has to offer.

As a MARC member, you’ll have access to a variety of engaging activities and resources, including regular meetings, special events, and hands-on community service projects. These events allow members to enhance their radio skills, contribute to emergency preparedness initiatives, and build meaningful connections within the amateur radio community. Joining MARC is a great way to grow personally while making a positive impact.

Interested in becoming a member? We'd love to have you! Feel free to reach out to our club secretary at secretary@mckinneyarc.org for more details. You can also visit our website at mckinneyarc.org to access a membership application and learn more about the benefits of joining, such as access to club resources, participation in exclusive events, and opportunities to collaborate with fellow operators.

If you're not ready to join just yet, why not attend one of our monthly meetings or public events? These gatherings provide a fantastic way to meet club members, ask questions, and experience the excitement of amateur radio firsthand.

Prefer to reach us by mail? Feel free to contact us at:

Secretary, McKinney Amateur Radio Club
P.O. Box 267
McKinney, TX 75070

We look forward to hearing from you and hope to see you at an upcoming meeting or event. Join us and become part of a passionate and welcoming community dedicated to the world of Amateur Radio!

Communications & Repeater Report

Presented by Ron Carlson (WW2CBI):

  • 75/380 Site (Water Tower): Operating perfectly. EchoLink connection is fully functional, hosted by Bob DeVance (K5CRX).
  • Custer/Virginia Site: Currently down. This VHF backup site is in the queue to be serviced following the Melissa and Fairview projects.
  • New Hope Site: The UHF repeater installation is waiting on a status update regarding a new tower installation. Ron has contacted the broadcast engineer for an update.
  • Melissa Water Tower: The water tower is undergoing a structural tank refurb and painting project. Temporary posts have been erected; the club's UHF repeater is temporarily operating at 60 feet (down from 170 feet) and showing surprisingly good propagation. Ground conduit (90 feet) has been successfully laid. Our Heliax will be stored at the city building near the water tower to be ready for installation.
  • A work party will be organized to pull the 1/2-inch and 7/8-inch Heliax cables through the conduit.

Special Project Update: Fairview Repeater

Dick Price (N5RKP) provided a comprehensive progress report on the parallel build-out of the Fairview site:

  • Container Build-Out: Framing, insulation, sheetrock, and painting were completed as of June 2nd. Moving shelves (donated by the Town of Fairview) have been installed. A relay rack has also been donated and will be set up to hold the upcoming equipment.
  • Electrical Work: Interior switches, outlets, and light strips were completed on June 6th. The Town of Fairview is providing the utility power and intends to eventually install a permanent backup generator at the site.
  • HVAC Mini-Split: Jeff Tucker (W5JLT) and Lance Chapman (KM6LMP) successfully mounted the unit. A replacement control board for the external unit arrived today and will be installed once permanent mains power is officially active.
  • Trenching and Conduit: On hold. Hand digging revealed that existing Rise Broadband cabinets were incorrectly placed directly on top of the town's leased conduits (40–48 inches deep). Rise Broadband has acknowledged the fault and is coordinating to physically relocate their cabinet.
  • Project Financials: Initial estimated cost ceiling was $23,159 (with $13,159 coming directly from MARC funds). Due to an extraordinary outpouring of member support — including 24 separate financial contributions totaling $15,219, alongside large material donations (paint, hardware) — the project currently has an estimated $5,000 net surplus.
  • Over a 23-day period, 14 separate club members have volunteered their manual labor for trenching, painting, and construction.

Presentation: Field Day 2026

Mike Flusche (W5MEF), Field Day Director, delivered the main presentation detailing the operational plan for the upcoming ARRL Field Day:

  • Dates: Saturday, June 27th – Sunday, June 28th, 2026.
  • Location: Anna Country RV Park. The venue offers air-conditioned operating rooms, indoor plumbing, a kitchen, and a separate space for VE testing.
  • Saturday Schedule: 7:00 AM breakfast on-site; 8:00 AM antenna tower setup (under Tower Master Dave Piekarski); 12:50 PM mandatory group photo (no lunch served until the photo is captured); 1:00 PM (1800 UTC) on-air operations begin; 2:00 PM VE testing session ($15 fee or $5 for youth; FRN and photo ID required); 4:30 PM fox hunting educational class (led by John Donovan, KC5JMD); 6:00 PM dinner and First Annual Chili Cookoff.
  • Chili Cookoff Rules: Pre-cooked at home, no kitchen prep, bring your own crockpot/heat source. Attendees receive two voting coupons to place in coffee cups assigned to each entry.
  • Classification: 4A NTX (four simultaneous transmitters, Category A Club Portable, North Texas Section).
  • Power Strategy: The goal is to run 100% on battery power to maximize emergency preparedness bonus points. Steve Ollmann has generously loaned two massive 200 Amp-Hour batteries to run the primary rigs. Rigs will utilize the club's existing Go-Kits (all Yaesu radios). Rigs must be on battery, and the digital laptop must be on independent power (Jackery/solar).
  • Stations A & B (Phone/Sideband): Supported by three 30-foot structural towers from John McDaniel (KI5VXP), placed near the entry driveway.
  • Station C (CW): Fed by a custom 68-foot mobile antenna trailer supporting an adjustable dipole. CW will use a Yaesu FT-991 (Larry Land, KI5OEZ).
  • Station D (Digital): Operating an End-Fed Half-Wave (EFHW) antenna wire set up in the adjacent grassy field.
  • Bonus Objectives: Complete a successful Winlink email transmit, copy the official W1AW Field Day bulletin, expand overall CW contacts (2 points per contact), and beat last year's total QSO scores.
  • Volunteers Needed: Heavy physical labor will be required for setup and takedown. Volunteers are required to bring gloves, hats, sunblock, bug spray, and closed-toe shoes (no flip-flops). Safety oversight will be managed by Roy DeLeon (KB4ROY).
  • Operator Scheduling: SignUpGenius is active on Discord and Groups.io. Operating slots are restricted to a maximum of two 1-hour blocks per person for the first week to allow equitable access. Restrictions lift next Tuesday at noon.

🏕️ Field Day 2026

MARC took to the field June 27–28 for ARRL Field Day 2026, operating class 4A NTX from our new home at the Anna Country RV Park — complete with air-conditioned operating rooms that made the Texas heat a whole lot friendlier. The results speak for themselves: we far exceeded last year's QSO total, with our CW operators alone pounding out more than 500 contacts.

The weekend was about more than contacts. Our Volunteer Examiner team, led by David Hampton (KI5LPO), held a testing session right at the event, and the club came away with three brand-new Amateur Extra operators and a new Technician. Congratulations to all — and a special tip of the hat to Bob DeVance (K5CRX), whose ten-week Extra prep class over Zoom helped make those upgrades happen.

Thank you to Field Day Director Mike Flusche (W5MEF) for months of planning, to Lance Chapman (KM6LMP) for securing the outstanding venue, and to every member who turned out for antenna setup, operating shifts, the chili cook-off, and tear-down. See you next year!

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR — GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services. Club members should utilize good judgment with regard to topics or content that are posted on the MARC reflector. To that end it is recommended that political and religious topics or content be avoided unless directly related to amateur radio. In addition, personal attacks in any form will not be tolerated.

McKinney testing is held at the Heard Natural Museum, 1 Nature Place, in McKinney, on the third Sunday of each month. Starting time is 2:00 PM and ends no later than 4:45pm (they close at 5:00 PM). Details are at k5rwk.org/ham-testing.

Sherman testing is administered each month just prior to the general meeting at 6:00 PM at the Texoma Council of Governments (TCOG), 1117 Gallagher Rd., Sherman, Texas. The general club meeting begins at 7:00 PM on the 3rd Tuesday of each month. ALL applicants MUST have their FRN (FCC Registration Number) handy — new applicants must acquire an FRN online prior to testing. No tests can be administered without an FRN. Application fee is $15 (cash only). You do not need to contact anyone prior to exam day — just be present at 6:00 PM with your FRN and testing fee. For more information contact: Ricky Jessen, KG5FDT, GCARC VE Lead, at testing@k5gcc.org (email preferred) or 903-868-2833.
