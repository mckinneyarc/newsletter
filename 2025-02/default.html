üé§ February Presenter - John Galvin, N5TIM

John Galvin, N5TIM, is an active member of the amateur radio community, with a strong focus on emergency communications and public service.

He is heavily involved in RACES (Radio Amateur Civil Emergency Service) and ARES (Amateur Radio Emergency Service) in the North Texas region. John‚Äôs dedication extends to organizing and participating in public service events in the Dallas/Fort Worth area. If you‚Äôre interested in volunteering for these events, visit www.qsl.net/n5tim for more information.

For those seeking information about Garland, Texas RACES/ARES activities, John encourages you to explore www.garlandraces.net. He is also available for inquiries about North Texas ARES and is always eager to support new and experienced operators in their amateur radio journeys.

John will provide an overview of Winlink, a worldwide radio email service that uses radio pathways where the internet is not available. Winlink provides email with attachments, position reporting, weather, and information bulletins. It is capable of operating completely without the internet, automatically forming a mesh network of radio links. It provides invaluable communications resources to individuals, radio stations, and groups.

üì° Pre-Programmed Radios Initiative

At the Oktoberfest celebration, McKinney club members discovered public interest in accessing emergency information via ham radio without requiring a license. In response, several club members are exploring ways to provide pre-programmed radios as a community service.

Pre-Programmed Radios

Tim (KK5TIM) noted that the club already has the necessary software and programming cables for multiple radio models. Joe (KJ5VYB) demonstrated this by programming a radio for a friend, providing an initial frequency list, disabling duplex to prevent unintentional transmissions, and suggesting programming radios at monthly sidewalk sales.

GMRS and FRS Opportunities

Peter (KJ5FJQ), Lance (KM6LMP), and Todd (KJ5INP) highlighted neighborhood interest in building a GMRS network. GMRS and FRS are seen as valuable tools for improving community communication. GMRS requires a license (no test needed) and covers the licensee's immediate family, serving as a gateway to ham radio for many.

Education and Community Engagement

Peter proposed educational initiatives to help individuals and neighborhoods understand emergency communication. Suggested topics include:

  • Differences between ham, GMRS, FRS, and CB radio
  • Understanding repeaters
  • Operating handheld transceivers

Bob (K5CRX) shared his HT class materials to support this effort.

Pre-Assembled Radio Kits

Geri (KI5OEX) has created sample ready-to-listen radio kits to simplify purchasing for newcomers. She assembled three kits using Baofeng K5-Plus tri-band radios, which display city names instead of frequencies. These radios are pre-programmed with UHF/VHF Skywarn repeaters, air bands, and GMRS/FRS channels.

Each kit includes:

  • Pre-programmed radio with battery
  • Signal stick antenna
  • Earpiece, wrist lanyard, charging dock, and USB-C cable
  • Laminated startup instructions, radio guide, and programming list
  • Packaged in an Apache 1800 case

Future Goals

The goal is to develop a replicable program for McKinney ARC, enabling neighborhoods to become "radio-ready" for emergencies. This is more a premium option and an example of what we could provide. Plans have not been finalized.

Garland testing is held on the fourth Thursday of each month and begins at 7:00 PM. Location is Freeman Heights Baptist Church, 1120 Garland Ave., Garland, TX 75040. Janet Crenshaw, WB9ZPH, at 972-302-9992 for details or wb9zph@gmail.com.

Plano testing is on the third Monday of each month at 6:00 PM. Testing sessions are located at 3160 East Spring Creek Parkway, Plano, TX. If you have questions, send an e-mail to Daryl, AF5QJ, at dm.af5qj@gmail.com.

Carrollton testing sessions are sponsored by the Metrocrest Amateur Radio Society (MARS). Testing is done both online and in-person. See kb5a.org for more details. For additional information, please contact Allan Batteiger at 972-672-0278 (wb5qng.org).

Sachse, Wylie, Murphy testing sessions are sponsored by the Sachse ARA. Testing is held at 9:00 AM the second Saturday of the month at the East Fork Masonic Lodge, 508 Parker Rd., Wylie, TX 75098. For more information, call 214-500-3472.

St. Paul testing sessions are sponsored by the PARK. Testing is held at 9:00 AM the third Saturday of the month at the St. Paul City Hall, 2505 Butcher's Block, St. Paul, TX 75098. Please contact Kerry A. Weeks at 972-442-1125.
