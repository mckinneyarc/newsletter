üé§ February Presenter - John Galvin, N5TIM

John Galvin, N5TIM, is an active member of the amateur radio community, with a strong focus on emergency communications and public service.

He is heavily involved in RACES (Radio Amateur Civil Emergency Service) and ARES (Amateur Radio Emergency Service) in the North Texas region. John‚Äôs dedication extends to organizing and participating in public service events in the Dallas/Fort Worth area. If you‚Äôre interested in volunteering for these events, visit www.qsl.net/n5tim for more information.

For those seeking information about Garland, Texas RACES/ARES activities, John encourages you to explore www.garlandraces.net. He is also available for inquiries about North Texas ARES and is always eager to support new and experienced operators in their amateur radio journeys.

John will provide an overview of Winlink, a worldwide radio email service that uses radio pathways where the internet is not available. Winlink provides email with attachments, position reporting, weather, and information bulletins. It is capable of operating completely without the internet, automatically forming a mesh network of radio links. It provides invaluable communications resources to individuals, radio stations, and groups.

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services.
