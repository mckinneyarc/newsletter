📢 President's Message

Hello MARC Family!

The shortest month of the year is now behind us, and February treated us to relatively mild weather compared to years past, so much so that much of it felt like spring already. With spring officially just a few weeks away, this is a great time to talk about being prepared for the rapidly changing weather we know all too well here in Texas.

Being ready for inclement weather, especially thunderstorms, starts with staying informed and planning ahead. Keep an eye on local forecasts and enable weather alerts on your phone so you're not caught off guard. Secure outdoor items that could become airborne in strong winds, and identify a safe place inside your home, away from windows, where you can take shelter if needed. It's also wise to keep an emergency kit stocked with essentials such as your amateur radio HTs, flashlights, batteries, water, medications, and a basic first-aid kit. Be sure to monitor our local repeaters for emergency information and SKYWARN reports. A little preparation goes a long way toward staying safe, calm, and protected when fast-developing Texas storms roll in.

With spring-like weather upon us, it's also a perfect time to get outside and enjoy the outdoors. One great way to do that is with a POTA outing, and speaking of that, the next MARC Potluck POTA event is officially on the calendar! Join us on Saturday, May 2nd, at the group pavilion at Bonham State Park. These events are always a blast, combining great food, fun on the air, and excellent camaraderie with friends. It's also a fantastic way to get warmed up for Field Day coming up in June, so be sure to mark your calendars now.

Spring is also a great season to tackle new projects, and we recently had an outstanding opportunity to support and encourage the next generation of innovators. A big thank-you goes to Bill Vining (N5YZ) for organizing MARC's participation in judging the Dallas Regional Science & Engineering Fair. In addition to Bill, MARC members Larry Land (KI5OEZ), Geri Sudman (KI5OEX), Luis Soto (KI5QAX), Melinda Keister (WZ5MEL), and myself, Robert Keister (WZ5V), served as judges. Two projects received certificate awards from MARC, and it was truly inspiring to witness the creativity, dedication, and hard work of hundreds of students presenting their projects.

And before diving into those springtime projects on a Saturday morning, don't forget one of our favorite traditions, breakfast together! Join us for our weekly Saturday morning breakfast at IHOP (US-75 & 380). Arrive around 7:00 AM to grab a seat, enjoy some pancakes and coffee, and catch up with fellow hams. It's the perfect way to start the weekend.

73,
Robert Keister – WZ5V
MARC President

🏆 Congratulations to Bob DeVance, K5CRX — ARRL North Texas Ham of the Year!

The McKinney Amateur Radio Club is thrilled to announce that our very own Bob DeVance, K5CRX, has been named the ARRL North Texas Section Ham of the Year for 2025. The award was presented at our January general meeting by ARRL North Texas Section Manager Steven Lott Smith, making it a truly memorable evening for all in attendance.

Bob's dedication to amateur radio and the North Texas ham community exemplifies what it means to be a well-rounded operator who gives back selflessly to both our hobby and the greater community.

A Legacy of Club Leadership

Bob is one of only six Life Members of the McKinney Amateur Radio Club—a distinction that cannot be purchased. To receive Life Member status, a member must have been active for at least 10 years, be nominated by a fellow member, and receive approval from the majority of the membership. Throughout his tenure, Bob has served in numerous leadership roles including President, Vice President, Communications Director, and Newsletter Editor.

Education and Mentorship

Bob has demonstrated a tireless dedication to educating hams and aspiring hams throughout North Texas. He has developed and delivered numerous high-quality classes, often providing all supplies, tools, and resources out of his own pocket. His sessions regularly attract up to 30 students eager to learn.

Some of the classes Bob has delivered include:

  • Electronics 101 series (DC Circuits, AC Circuits, Solid State Devices, Digital Principles)
  • HT Basics (UHF/VHF)
  • Scout Merit Badge instruction at the annual McKinney Merit Badge Boot Camp
  • Operating Modes workshops

Bob delivers these classes to anyone who wants to learn, regardless of club affiliation or license status, helping prepare our community for advancement while giving them practical skills in electronic and communication technology.

Technical Excellence

Bob has been the principal driver behind many of the technical improvements to the MARC repeater infrastructure, including:

  • Configuring and implementing EchoLink integration for the MARC 2m repeater system
  • Configuring and implementing Wires-X integration for the MARC 70cm repeater system
  • Implementing SCOM repeater controllers across multiple repeaters
  • Deploying multiple Yaesu DR-2X repeaters within the MARC infrastructure
  • Hosting the Wires-X and EchoLink gateways at his home QTH

These contributions extend far beyond MARC—our repeater infrastructure is a key component of the Collin County ARES plans, activities, and support of the National Weather Service SKYWARN® program.

Inspiring the Next Generation

Bob's commitment to youth extends through his work with Scouting programs and his leadership of MARC's GOTA (Get On The Air) stations during Field Day each year. Through these efforts, he has introduced countless young people to the wonder of amateur radio. Bob has also been an enthusiastic participant in MARC's sponsorship of scholarships at the Dallas Area Regional Science Fair, serving on judging panels and contributing to the MARC Youth Fund.

A Well-Deserved Honor

In recognition of this achievement, Bob was also presented with a MARC Challenge Coin—only the second ever issued by our club.

Bob exemplifies the best qualities of an amateur radio operator. His professionalism, knowledge, generosity, and willingness to invest his time, energy, and resources inspire us all. Please join us in congratulating Bob on this well-deserved recognition!

73, Bob — and thank you for everything you do for MARC and the amateur radio community!

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR — GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services. Club members should utilize good judgment with regard to topics or content that are posted on the MARC reflector. To that end it is recommended that political and religious topics or content be avoided unless directly related to amateur radio. In addition, personal attacks in any form will not be tolerated.
