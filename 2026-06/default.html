📢 President's Message

Hello MARC Members,

I hope everyone had a wonderful Memorial Day and took time to remember and honor the brave men and women who made the ultimate sacrifice in service to our country and the freedoms we enjoy today.

Over the holiday weekend, MARC was once again proud to support the Smiles Charity. We received tremendous positive feedback from the organization regarding the assistance our club provided. A special thank you goes to Sid (K5DKW), for coordinating our participation, and to all the volunteers who generously gave their time and effort to make the event a success. Your dedication reflects the very best of what MARC represents.

It's the month of June! That means one of the most anticipated events of the year is just around the corner—Field Day! We are only a few weeks away, and excitement is building. This year brings a couple of major improvements: a new location and air conditioning! We're looking forward to another fantastic weekend of operating, learning, fellowship, and fun. If you're a newer member, we especially encourage you to join us and experience one of amateur radio's premier events firsthand. Our most awesome Field Day director, Michael (W5MEF), will give us all the details at the next meeting.

I would also like to extend a sincere thank you to Dick (N5RKP), for leading the Fairview Repeater Project. Thanks to his leadership and the hard work of many dedicated club members, the equipment container is nearing completion. The progress made so far has been remarkable, and it is a testament to what can be accomplished when our members come together toward a common goal. A project update will be provided at our next club meeting as we work toward completing the remaining tasks.

One of the things that makes MARC special is the sense of family we share. When one member experiences hardship, the entire club feels it. Please join me in extending our heartfelt condolences to Lance (KM6LMP), on the loss of his father on May 28th. Many members have already shared their thoughts and prayers through our Health and Welfare channel on Discord. If you ever find yourself facing difficult circumstances and need support, please don't hesitate to reach out. Our club is here for you.

One of my favorite MARC traditions continues to be our Saturday morning breakfasts at IHOP in McKinney. If you haven't joined us yet, we would love to see you there. While it may require an early start to the weekend, the great conversations, laughter, and camaraderie make it well worth the effort.

Thank you for being a valued MARC member and for helping make this club such a welcoming, active, and vibrant community. I look forward to seeing many of you at Field Day and at our upcoming events throughout the summer.

73,
— Robert Keister, WZ5V

🌍 Join Us!

The McKinney Amateur Radio Club (MARC) welcomes everyone, whether you're just discovering the world of Amateur Radio or you're an experienced operator looking to connect with fellow enthusiasts. Our club is open to anyone with a passion for radio communication, a curiosity for learning, or a desire to expand their skills. Amateur Radio is a fascinating, educational, and rewarding hobby, and we invite you to explore everything MARC has to offer.

As a MARC member, you’ll have access to a variety of engaging activities and resources, including regular meetings, special events, and hands-on community service projects. These events allow members to enhance their radio skills, contribute to emergency preparedness initiatives, and build meaningful connections within the amateur radio community. Joining MARC is a great way to grow personally while making a positive impact.

Interested in becoming a member? We'd love to have you! Feel free to reach out to our club secretary at secretary@mckinneyarc.org for more details. You can also visit our website at mckinneyarc.org to access a membership application and learn more about the benefits of joining, such as access to club resources, participation in exclusive events, and opportunities to collaborate with fellow operators.

If you're not ready to join just yet, why not attend one of our monthly meetings or public events? These gatherings provide a fantastic way to meet club members, ask questions, and experience the excitement of amateur radio firsthand.

Prefer to reach us by mail? Feel free to contact us at:

We look forward to hearing from you and hope to see you at an upcoming meeting or event. Join us and become part of a passionate and welcoming community dedicated to the world of Amateur Radio!

Main Technical Presentation

  • Speaker: Bob DeVance (K5CRX), North Texas Ham Radio Operator of the Year.
  • Topic: "How to Burn Your Fingers in One Easy Step: A Guide to Practical Electronics Soldering."
  • Emphasized the pride and fun of DIY electronic assembly.
  • Safety: static control (grounding wrist straps), proper ventilation/fume extractors for rosin core smoke, and mandatory eye protection.
  • Compared equipment: pencil irons (30W) vs. soldering guns (Weller 100/140W for outdoor coax and heavy sheet metal shields) vs. modern digital temperature-controlled stations and portable butane-fired units.
  • Soldering chemistry: 60/40 lead-tin vs. 63/37 eutectic solder (instant solidifying to prevent cold joints) and the challenges of high-temperature lead-free solder required by European standards.
  • Demonstrated modern desoldering: Solder-Wick, manual solder suckers, dental picks for clearing PC board holes, and hot-air rework stations for Surface Mount Technology (SMT) components.
  • Presented a historical NASA space shuttle video clip showing zero-gravity rosin flux behavior on melting solder.

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR — GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services. Club members should utilize good judgment with regard to topics or content that are posted on the MARC reflector. To that end it is recommended that political and religious topics or content be avoided unless directly related to amateur radio. In addition, personal attacks in any form will not be tolerated.
