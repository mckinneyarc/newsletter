üé§ March Presenter - Erik Jacobsen, NQ5Z

Erik Jacobsen, NQ5Z, has been a dedicated amateur radio operator since 1988. His passion for emergency communications has taken him across the globe, from serving in the USAF in Germany‚Äîwhere he operated under DA1JE and AFT7SP‚Äîto deploying for disaster recovery efforts, including Hurricane Hugo relief in Puerto Rico and the US Virgin Islands in 1989, as well as emergency communications during the 1992 Chicago flood.

A lifelong advocate for public service and preparedness, Erik is an active member of the ARRL, Collin County ARES, and has previously served with McHenry County ARES and Lake County RACES. His dedication to emergency communications and Skywarn underscores his commitment to helping communities stay connected when it matters most.

Beyond emergency communications, Erik is involved in promoting amateur radio to the next generation. He has actively supported Jamboree on the Air (JOTA), running a station for scout troops and inspiring young operators to explore the world of radio. He is currently pursuing the Worked All States Award and enjoys operating on D-Star.

A strong proponent of Morse code, Erik continues to enjoy CW operations and encourages newcomers to embrace its unique challenges and rewards. While he respects all aspects of amateur radio, he fondly recalls the teachings of his elmer, WB9CYI, who championed CW proficiency and its enduring value in the hobby.

Whether on the airwaves or mentoring new operators, Erik‚Äôs enthusiasm for amateur radio remains as strong as ever. Join us in welcoming him to share his experiences and insights at our upcoming meeting!
