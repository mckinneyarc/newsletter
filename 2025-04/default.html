üåç Join Us!

The McKinney Amateur Radio Club (MARC) welcomes everyone, whether you're just discovering the world of Amateur Radio or you're an experienced operator looking to connect with fellow enthusiasts. Our club is open to anyone with a passion for radio communication, a curiosity for learning, or a desire to expand their skills. Amateur Radio is a fascinating, educational, and rewarding hobby, and we invite you to explore everything MARC has to offer.

As a MARC member, you‚Äôll have access to a variety of engaging activities and resources, including regular meetings, special events, and hands-on community service projects. These events allow members to enhance their radio skills, contribute to emergency preparedness initiatives, and build meaningful connections within the amateur radio community. Joining MARC is a great way to grow personally while making a positive impact.

Interested in becoming a member? We'd love to have you! Feel free to reach out to our club secretary at secretary@mckinneyarc.org for more details. You can also visit our website at mckinneyarc.org to access a membership application and learn more about the benefits of joining, such as access to club resources, participation in exclusive events, and opportunities to collaborate with fellow operators.

If you're not ready to join just yet, why not attend one of our monthly meetings or public events? These gatherings provide a fantastic way to meet club members, ask questions, and experience the excitement of amateur radio firsthand.

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR ‚Äî GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services.
