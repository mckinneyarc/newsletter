üó≥Ô∏è Voting Procedures for 2026 Election of Board of Directors

Since we only have elections once per year, we thought it would be a good idea to remind everyone of the procedures and processes for the conduct of the Board of Directors election. The MARC Constitution and By-Laws have some specific rules and processes that must be followed:

Eligibility to Vote ‚Äì To be eligible to vote in the election, you must be an active member of the club (i.e. dues are current) and must have attended at least one regular meeting either in-person, via Zoom, or by Proxy within the six months preceding the election. This is one of the reasons why we try to be so careful about recording attendance at our regular meetings.

Voting by Proxy ‚Äì If you are eligible to vote, but cannot attend the November meeting either in person or via Zoom, you can assign your Proxy to another eligible voting member to cast your vote for you. To do this, you will need to send an email to Lance Chapman, KM6LMP stating that you want to assign your Proxy to another eligible voting member. In your email, you will need to identify the eligible voting member to whom you will be assigning your Proxy. Be sure that you have also notified the person to whom you are assigning your Proxy so they will know that they will be casting your vote along with their own. It is important to note, that no eligible voting member may cast more than two Proxy votes in addition to their own.

Nominations ‚Äì The Nominating Committee will be presenting their nominations for Board positions at the beginning of the voting process. You can see who they are recommending before the meeting by reviewing the sample ballot that is contained within this edition of the newsletter. During the meeting, the President will also call for any further nominations to come from the floor. If you want to run for a position, or if you want to nominate someone else for a position, this will be the time to do so. Nominations from the floor will require a second and a vote of the membership to place the nominee on the ballot.

Voting ‚Äì Eligible voting members physically in attendance will be able to cast their vote and any Proxy votes they are carrying during the meeting. For those attending via Zoom, we will set up a "poll" within the Zoom to allow remote eligible voting members to cast their vote. Should it occur that all of the Board positions have only a single nominee each, the President will ask for an eligible voting member to make a motion to elect the slate of nominees by acclimation. This will negate the need for the issuance, collection, or counting of any paper ballots.

We hope that this article will help everyone to better understand the process. If you have any questions, please feel free to email Robert Keister ‚Äì WZ5V at president@mckinneyarc.org

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR ‚Äî GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services. Club members should utilize good judgment with regard to topics or content that are posted on the MARC reflector. To that end it is recommended that political and religious topics or content be avoided unless directly related to amateur radio. In addition, personal attacks in any form will not be tolerated.
