ü¶ä Say Hello to Steve!

Felicia has a new partner for you to hunt on weekends and his name is Steve. I‚Äôve built Steve to provide Fox Hunters another reason to get out and enjoy this fun activity. We now have twice the Foxes and twice the opportunity to perfect our radio direction finding skills!

Steve operates at 433.750Mhz FM and wakes just after the top of every 3rd minute (:00, :03, :06, :09, etc) and provides 40-50secs of message, which includes a CW preamble and closing with some audio content in-between. His normal operating hours are Saturday 0700 to 2000 and Sunday 0700 to 1800-ish ‚Äì weather permitting. A slight drizzle won‚Äôt bother him, but much more than that and he‚Äôll have to come in from the rain. Any changes to the schedule will be posted in the MARC Discord::foxhunt channel.

You will normally find Steve in western McKinney or eastern Prosper, although he may wander elsewhere within the McKinney boundaries, where you‚Äôll normally find Felicia. There‚Äôs plenty of room for both.

For those that are interested in what‚Äôs in the box ‚Äì Steve is powered by a Raspberry Pi single-board computer with RealTimeClock module and home-brew interface board that provides connections to a QuanSheng UVK5 hand-held. RF output is currently set to ‚ÄúMedium‚Äù (2.0-2.5w) and the whole thing is powered by a 20Ah LiFePO4 battery, so he has plenty of reserve. We may decide to change the RF output at a later time, but for now my preference is to be sure that he can be heard over a wider area.

Like Felicia, Steve is enclosed in a green ‚Äòammo box‚Äô displaying a QR code for you to scan, directing you to our Fox Log Entry page ‚Äì where you‚Äôll record your name, callsign, and other information. Be sure to leave a comment on how you found the hunt ‚Ä¶ but don‚Äôt give away too many clues.

Our logging application keeps track of everyone that successfully finds each Fox and we map all of the locations our Foxes have been placed (after the hunt has been closed). The map is at https://kc5jmd.net/fox-manager-map-w5mrc/ Felicia is indicated with red markers, Steve with green. Click on the markers for more information on the hunt.

We hope you take the time to get out and hunt these Foxes ‚Äì both Felicia (433.500) and Steve (433.750) on the weekends. The FoxMasters do our very best to ensure the Foxes are out in the field every weekend and hope you make good use of them. We‚Äôd love to hear your stories from the hunt or any suggestions you have for making the experience even better. Feel free to share them on the MARC Discord::foxhunt channel.

Hello, my name is Doug KI5QED, and I am writing this for the purpose of helping fellow Hams with a common problem we all have. Not enough time to spend with our radio at home! As we see the median age of Ham Radio operators trending down, it is a very exciting thing to watch! However, most younger operators have regular jobs we must all attend these days, must keep that radio money coming in! So, the availability of spending time in front of our rigs at home is preciously small from day to day. The software I will be describing in this article is designed for this specific problem! Remote transmissions are not a new thing to Ham Radio; stations have had this same need since the beginning of Amateur Radio! What I have found makes the process a bit easier, and the experience more enjoyable!

I live in Princeton but work in Little Elm. The dealership I work for is very understanding about my passion for Ham Radio. I have an Allstar radio-less node on my desk with an ethernet cable for connection! That allows me the opportunity to connect around the world on many repeaters and hub networks.

But I still found myself in need of access to my home shack to monitor local frequencies I have found. The Remote TX software gave me this ability! For more information on this, you can visit https://remotetx.net

So, how to remote in was the question I started with. And I will be honest; the answers were more confusing than the problem! I knew I would have to have something on the radio I could log into for radio control. A raspberry Pi would be the right thing, but how to control that is where the real confusion started! There are many ways to get into that machine and make it work for you, and if you are knowledgeable with programming I am told it is not too hard to get done. That was just not the best scenario for my taste. Remote TX has made the process as streamlined as possible. You get a raspberry Pi, burn a customized image onto the SD card, and hook it up to your radio with a simple USB cord! Sounds simple, but during my setup I found a few things were not as planned at my shack!

Security was a big problem I had to address early on! I wanted a system that only I could log into. And while that was possible with a home brew system, my confidence level was not well placed with that option. Remote TX provides all the cloud server security, and an easy path for me with encryption to access my radio! After resizing the pathway for everything in my shack, I found the setup process to be as easy as described on the website! I signed up for the thirty-day free trial and when I got the email that my account was ready, I downloaded the Balena Etcher software, customized my image with my callsign, and burned the image on the micro-SD card very quickly! After booting up the Pi, and hooking it up to the radio, I logged into the webpage and there we were!

The remote software does not allow for a waterfall display on my Yeasu 991A, but several of the Icom radios do offer that. With the software, I can do anything with my radio otherwise. I can switch bands, and tune, as well and repeater encoding just as if I were there in front of my shack radio! The webpage software uses your speakers and microphone, and I have had success using my iPad with a Bluetooth earbud. There are several settings you need to configure to get this working. The website has plenty of quick start guides that help with these depending on your radio choice! This was the biggest problem I faced, but customer service through email with Remote TX was wonderful! They had me figured out and up and running in no time! You may also try the trucker‚Äôs Bluetooth with a microphone that wraps around to the mouth, it will be something I try in the next few days to see how the sound is best produced!

Just to make you aware, there is of course a cost involved with this service. As of writing this it is only $70/yr. they do offer a $40/ 6mo as well, if you‚Äôre just looking to feel it out. Something I am doing with the thirty-day free trial! I will be purchasing the software for at least a year to see how much I use it. But I have the feeling it will be a permanent tool I keep on hand!
