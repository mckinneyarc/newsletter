üìú Proposed Bylaws Amendment ‚Äì Communications Director Technical Assistants

The MARC membership is being asked to consider a proposed amendment to Section 10, Paragraph 3 of the bylaws. The purpose of this change is to allow the Communications Director to appoint additional Technical Assistants as needed, beyond the current limit of two. This amendment was introduced at the September General Meeting and will be brought to a vote at the October General Meeting.

Current Bylaw Language

The Communications Director can request two (2) Technical Assistants for the maintenance of the Club repeaters, antennas, and other radio equipment. As in the case of the Communications Director, the nominating committee will also qualify these members. In the interest of technical continuity, the terms of these positions should overlap.

Motion

At the September meeting, the following motion was introduced:

‚ÄúI make a motion that we strike the number two (2) and leave the rest of it as is.‚Äù

The motion was made by Dave Piekarski (K5EEN), tabled in accordance with procedure, and is published here for membership review prior to the October vote.

Proposed Bylaw Language

The Communications Director can request Technical Assistants for the maintenance of the Club repeaters, antennas, and other radio equipment. As in the case of the Communications Director, the nominating committee will also qualify these members. In the interest of technical continuity, the terms of these positions should overlap.

Rationale

When the bylaws were adopted, MARC maintained only a single 2-meter repeater. Today, the club oversees a far more complex infrastructure, including:

  • Two 2-meter repeaters (analog and digital)
  • One 70cm repeater (analog and digital)
  • WiresX and EchoLink gateways
  • Two APRS digipeaters
  • Four repeater sites (75/380, Custer/Virginia, Melissa, and New Hope)
  • Construction underway for a second 70cm DMR repeater with microwave video link in Fairview

This scale and complexity requires more resources than originally anticipated. Removing the numerical limit allows the Communications Director to appoint Technical Assistants with specific expertise across MARC‚Äôs integrated infrastructure.

Next Steps

The amendment will be voted on at the October General Meeting. A simple majority of eligible voting members present is required for approval. Members are encouraged to review the proposed change and rationale in advance.
