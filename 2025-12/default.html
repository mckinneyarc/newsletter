üéÑ MARC Christmas Party & Gift Exchange

MARC will host its annual Christmas Party with Gift Exchange on December 9 at 7:00 PM at Spring Creek Barbeque. This event has always been one of the most popular and well attended events that MARC supports.

The club will be providing $500 worth of gifts for club members that will be awarded via a drawing. Following the drawings for club-provided gifts, the annual gift exchange will take front and center. This is a fun time with members selecting a gift, opening the selected gift, and perhaps having it taken by another member. Of course, if the gift is taken, he/she may take another gift that has been opened or select another gift from the pool of gifts.

The board is recommending that a gift of approximately $25 in value be purchased, wrapped, and brought to the party where it will be placed on a table in front of all attendees for all to see. The board is encouraging that all participants bring a gift they would like to receive ‚Äì in other words ‚Äì no junk.

Please note that the club-provided gifts are for club members in good standing only. If one does not want to participate in the gift exchange, he/she is still welcome at the Christmas Party but would simply not bring a gift for the exchange.

Christmas Gift Exchange Rules

  • Each player who wishes to participate in the exchange brings one wrapped gift (suggested value $25 ‚Äì in other words, a gift you would like to receive) to contribute to a common pool. If you elect not to participate, simply sit out the exchange and enjoy the show.
  • Each player will be provided a numbered ticket to determine the order in which players will go.
  • The gift pool will be located at the front of the room for all to see.
  • Ticket numbers will be drawn randomly to determine order of gift selection. The first player selects a gift from the pool and opens it. He/she should make sure everyone can see the gift!
  • Subsequent players can choose to either pick an unwrapped gift from the pool or take any previous player's gift. Anyone who gets their gift taken in this way can do the same ‚Äì choose a new gift from the pool or take one from someone else.
    • Limit on gift swapping: Three swaps and the gift is out. If a present is taken three times (it is now in the hands of its fourth owner), it's out of the exchange and the person who holds it gets to walk away with it unless the first player wants it at the end of the exchange.
  • After all players have had a turn, the first player gets a chance to swap the gift he/she is holding for any other opened gift. Anyone who's gift is taken may swap gifts with someone else (as long as that person hasn't been taken from yet). When someone declines to swap gifts, the exchange comes to an end.

We look forward to celebrating the season with you at this festive event. Bring your holiday spirit, a great gift, and get ready for an evening of fellowship and fun!

Presentation by Tim Valis KK5TIM

Antenna Field Testing & Results Review

Purpose: Compare SWR and real-world performance of various portable antennas using FT8 and PSK Reporter.

1. Overview

  • Eight antennas were assembled and tested, including a Delta Loop, several verticals, POTA Performers, the Chelegance MC-750, the MyGoTo antenna, a Radiodity antenna, the Ultimax, and a Yaesu ATAS-120.
  • The goal was to review SWR, deployment ease, and FT8 propagation results across all antennas.

2. Test Method

  • All antennas were measured on the 20-meter band using a RigExpert AA-230 analyzer.
  • SWR was recorded at lower band edge, center, upper band edge, and lowest point.
  • FT8 was used to transmit three CQ cycles per antenna over 90 seconds.
  • PSK Reporter was used as the reverse beacon network to map how far each antenna was heard.
  • A single radio, wattmeter, and antenna switch were used to ensure consistent testing.

3. Key Findings

  • Most antennas had acceptable SWR and could operate without a tuner.
  • Some antennas could not handle 100 W digital:
    • Radiodity, MyGoTo, and ATAS-120 needed power reduced to 25 W.
  • Several antennas reached Europe, Alaska, and Asia via FT8.
  • Roger's POTA Performer achieved the farthest reach, including Japan.
  • SWR quality did not always correlate with best propagation results.
  • The Chelegance, Delta Loop, and well-tuned POTA Performers performed strongly.

4. Issues Encountered

  • Misidentified and unlabeled cables caused confusion during antenna switching.
  • Overpowering low-rated antennas caused temporary shutdowns but no damage.
  • The ATAS-120 required extra ground radials and long auto-tune time.
  • Wind affected at least one antenna (POTA Performer), requiring additional ballast.
  • Some PSK Reporter logs were lost due to the 24-hour retention limit.

5. Observations

  • Deployment height and radial setup made noticeable performance differences.
  • The recent solar disturbance (CME) reduced expected band conditions, yet antennas still achieved global reach.
  • Setup differences between two POTA Performers produced up to 50% difference in spots received.

6. Lessons Learned

  • Label all cables at both ends before testing.
  • Respect antenna power limits, especially with 100%-duty-cycle digital modes.
  • Allow sufficient time for ATAS autotrim tuning.
  • SWR is only one factor‚Äîtuning and deployment strongly influence performance.
  • Take more photos for better documentation next time.

7. Future Plans

  • Another antenna comparison event is planned for spring.
  • Additional refinements and a larger field layout may be used.
  • Testing may continue during Winter Field Day.

MARC OPERATES OPEN REPEATERS ON 146.740 AND 443.200 MHZ

EchoLink is provided on 146.740 by connecting through W5MRC-R. Regular/frequent users of the repeater are encouraged to join the club and be an active part of the local Ham community, as well as helping to support the repeaters‚Äô continued operation.

The MARC information net is held every Sunday at 8:00 PM CT, on 146.740 MHz.

Collin County ARES holds a training net on the third Sunday of each month at 9:00 PM CT on 146.740 MHz.

The MARC simplex net is held every Monday at 8:00 PM CT, on 146.540 MHz.

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR ‚Äî GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services. Club members should utilize good judgment with regard to topics or content that are posted on the MARC reflector. To that end it is recommended that political and religious topics or content be avoided unless directly related to amateur radio. In addition, personal attacks in any form will not be tolerated.
