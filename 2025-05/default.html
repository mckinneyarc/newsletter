üé∏ Smiles Charity Concert Help!

We have been asked again to help with traffic control at the Smiles Charity concert this year. To the best of my memory this is the 10th year MARC has teamed up with Smiles Charity to benefit a deserving veteran and their family. The event will be May 25th, Sunday of Memorial Day weekend. We will start around 3:30 PM and wrap up around 8:00 PM. Who is Smiles Charity you ask?

Our Mission

Bringing the Community Together to Change the World One Home, One Family at a Time.

Smiles Charity utilizes a unique fundraising concept of bringing the community together for a concert to provide mortgage-free homes for wounded veterans. Generous local and corporate businesses sponsor the annual concert event held over Memorial Day weekend in McKinney, TX. 100% of the net profits from the annual event are then donated to pay off mortgages for wounded veterans.

In its first three years, Smiles Charity benefitted Habitat for Humanity and helped build three homes for three amazing McKinney families who are beating the odds and now living the dream of home ownership. In 2011, Smiles Charity was honored to partner with an organization to help fund the build of homes for severely wounded veterans. Since then, Smiles Charity has continued to support disabled veterans by providing a mortgage-free home each year.

The results are tangible. Since 2008, 22 mortgage-free homes have been provided through the generous donations of our sponsors. Each year past recipients attend the event and are able to interact with the sponsors that have provided their homes. Sponsors can see first-hand how their contributions are making an impact. *

This is a very worthwhile and rewarding opportunity to give back to our veterans and our community. If you would like to know more or sign up contact me at w5kzjham@gmail.com. I will also ask for a show of hands of past participants at the next meeting so you will have the opportunity to ask them as well.

üçñ Potluck POTA BBQ at Bonham State Park

The McKinney Amateur Radio Club hosted a successful Potluck POTA BBQ at Bonham State Park on Saturday, April 19th, 2025. We gathered at Group Picnic Pavilion 2 with plenty of parking and restrooms nearby, making it an ideal location for a full day of fun, food, and radio.

Attendees brought family, friends, radio gear, and of course, delicious food! A highlight of the day was a slow-smoked pork loin with BBQ fixings, which paired perfectly with the many potluck sides shared by members. The atmosphere was casual, friendly, and full of great conversation.

The event was open to all operators and their families, encouraging members to bring spouses and children. Newcomers and guests interested in amateur radio were welcomed warmly. These community-focused events are a great way to introduce others to our hobby and showcase its relevance and fun.

While Texas State Park entry fees were in effect, those with a State Parks Pass (or veterans) enjoyed free entry. If you're into POTA or camping, the pass is a great investment.

For those with a bit of mic fright, this event provided a relaxed environment to get on the air and build confidence. Several members used this as a chance to practice making contacts and learning the ropes, perfect preparation for Field Day.

A fun twist to the day was ‚ÄúFelicia the Fox,‚Äù who made an appearance and led several members on a light-hearted fox hunt challenge using their HTs on 433.500 MHz.

Thank you to all who joined us!

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR ‚Äî GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services. Club members should utilize good judgment with regard to topics or content that are posted on the MARC reflector. To that end it is recommended that political and religious topics or content be avoided unless directly related to amateur radio. In addition, personal attacks in any form will not be tolerated.
