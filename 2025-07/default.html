üìò The First Electronics 101 Class Well-Attended

The first MARC Electronics 101 class for 2025 convened at McKinney Fire Station # 10 on June 14th to tackle DC Circuits, the first lesson in the series with more to come. The 18 attendees explored the wonders of direct current, lamps, resistors, switches, breadboard wiring, Ohm‚Äôs Law, and use of the Volt-Ohm-Meter. All the classes in the Electronics series are ‚Äúhands-on‚Äù and provide an opportunity for participants to get their hands dirty as they wire up circuits to demonstrate the principles studied. Fun learning was the order of the day.

The good mix of new and experienced hams worked especially well, allowing practiced hands to assist those who were hesitant to flip the power switch to see if the circuit really worked. MARC members are the greatest at pitching in to help each other and this class illustrates it vividly.

If you were unable to attend or need a refresh, keep an eye on our YouTube channel for the video presentation to be posted soon.

There will be six classes in the series covering DC circuits, AC circuits, Solid State Devices, Vacuum Tubes, Digital Principles, and one wild card for a class or a project build. Watch the McKinney Amatuer Radio Club‚Äôs Discord ‚ÄòEvents‚Äô channel and sign up from groups IO emails so you don‚Äôt miss a class.

The AC circuits class is scheduled for 9am July 12th at McKinney Fire Station #10 1150 Olympic Crossing. Whether you are a novice or a mentor, we would love for you to join us!
