üé§ January Featured Speaker - William Newman, WL9N

William Newman, WL9N, is an accomplished amateur radio operator and seasoned emergency communications expert. With years of experience in both the Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES) and the Radio Amateur Civil Emergency Service (RACES), William has become a trusted leader in the field of emergency and disaster communications.

In 2009, William completed the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Communications Unit Leader (COML) coursework and was later certified by the State of Illinois as an All-Hazards Type III Communications Unit Leader. His expertise led him to the prestigious Emergency Management Institute (EMI) in Maryland, where he earned his COML Instructor's Certification in 2012.

William‚Äôs HAM radio journey began with a passion for DXing and antenna experimentation. His original HF station was powered by an Icom 7700 and a True Talk G5RV dipole antenna.

Since relocating to the Dallas-Fort Worth area, William has faced antenna restrictions but remains determined to find innovative solutions to stay active on the airwaves. Known for his love of traditional QSL card exchanges, he cherishes the connections made through HAM radio and always encourages operators to send cards the ‚Äúold-fashioned way.‚Äù

Join us as William shares his expertise on joining Texas State RACES and navigating the State Emergency Response process, providing invaluable insights into the critical role amateur radio plays in disaster preparedness and response.

Let‚Äôs give Mr. Newman a warm welcome!

üåê Mobile Public Service Deployment Committee (MPSDC) - End-of-Year Update

Committee Objectives

The committee reviewed the necessity of replacing the outgoing CSU trailer and concluded that a replacement was not needed.

It also assessed the needs of the communities served, identifying events such as Oktoberfest, bike races, and other gatherings that require Base and HT UHF/VHF/Simplex communications.

Additionally, the committee explored options for a new platform, emphasizing the importance of lighter weight kits to enhance portability and efficiency.

Summary of Activities

We started off the year with a club-approved decision to sell the MPSDC trailer. The trailer has been sold, and the funds are now in the club account.

With no emergency equipment demands from local governments and ARES having a defined list for self-deployment, we focused on UHF/VHF and HF kits. These kits can be used by the club at various events.

UHF/VHF Portable Go Box

We developed a UHF/VHF portable go box consisting of a Yaesu FTM-300 dual-band radio, chosen partly for its digital capability with WiresX. The assembly includes:

  • An onboard 20 Ah battery
  • USB/voltmeter power & antenna port
  • A dual-band antenna (external antenna option available)
  • Anderson PowerPole connections for external power
  • A charging port and a switch between battery and external power

The radio is mounted using HDPE plastic for the radio mount and battery cover, all enclosed in an Apache 3800 box.

HF Portable Deployment Kit

Significant time was spent on the design and layout of a new HF box for deployment, club activities, and field day.

The previous cases weighed 75 lbs and required two people to carry. Our goal was to limit the weight to no more than 35 lbs for easier portability.

The new design incorporates the FlexStack box system from Lowes. The cabinet box opens from the bottom and slides out of the way, while the roller box is used to store the battery, coax, headset, and other accessories, ensuring easy maneuverability.

A Yaesu FT-710 transceiver was chosen for the HF rig, along with a sound mixer for multiple audio devices. Panel mounts allow connections on the back of the radio to remain accessible when the box is opened.

Prototype Design

The first working prototype includes a frame constructed with 2020 extruded aluminum to securely hold components in place and an HDPE front panel to provide a clean and polished appearance.

The lower box is designed to house the battery, charger, power supply, headphones, remote keypad, remote PTT, and miscellaneous cables. The system is equipped with wheels and a handle to ensure easy portability.

Connection Features

The kit includes the following connections:

  • USB and USB 2.0 (printer style)
  • CW key and remote keyer for voice memory and frequency selection
  • Anderson PowerPole connections
  • USB/voltmeter for phone charging
  • Antenna connector

The radio slides on frame rails to the front of the box, with the prototype weighing in at 32 lbs. The next step is selecting antennas to complete the kit.

The final unit is planned to be presented at the December Board of Directors meeting for review and approval.

MARC E-MAIL REFLECTOR ‚Äî GROUPS.IO

MARC maintains a chat group on groups.io to facilitate club communication between meetings and newsletters. Sign up for this service by visiting https://groups.io/g/McKinneyARC. Your application will be approved quickly.

The purposes of the MARC email reflector are: 1) Facilitate the timely distribution of MARC information and news to club members. 2) Allow for the sharing of amateur radio related topics among club members. 3) Provide a forum for the discussion of amateur related information among club members. 4) Act as a marketplace for the buying and selling of amateur radio and computer equipment and services. Club members should utilize good judgment with regard to topics or content that are posted on the MARC reflector. To that end it is recommended that political and religious topics or content be avoided unless directly related to amateur radio. In addition, personal attacks in any form will not be tolerated.
